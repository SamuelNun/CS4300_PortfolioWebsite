The Many Platforms of Steam, Alternatives To PC Gaming

Valve has spent the last decade experimenting with ways to bring PC gaming into more accessible and console-like formats. Each project pushed the idea forward, eventually leading to hardware that resonated with millions of players. Valve maintains a monopoly over the PC space and honestly, I personally wouldn't have it any other way. This is a company that seeks to innovate no matter what competition may try to challenge them, maintaining one of the most respected companies ever created through it's creations and company philosphies.

Steam Machine – A Idea Ahead of Its Time Comes Back Again

Initially launched in 2015, the Steam Machine was Valve’s first hardware push. These compact PCs running SteamOS were meant to bring PC gaming to the living room but struggled due to compatibility issues and pricing. In recent times, they try once again with the Steam Machine as Valve maintains a monopoly over the PC sphere, a sphere significantly more popular than it ever was back in the initial launch.

Steam Deck – Valve’s Breakthrough Success

Released in 2022, the Steam Deck became an instant hit. It combined PC power with handheld convenience and introduced Proton compatibility, making thousands of Windows games playable on Linux. The success of the Steam Deck led to a boom in PC-handheld gaming and interest in PCs in general as the Steam Deck was more than just a PC-hanheld, with it being able to dock to a monitor and be utilized as a full fledge desktop with it's competent hardware and accessibility.

Steam Frame – The Next Step in Valve’s Vision

The Steam Frame is a simple, affordable streaming device meant for living-room gaming. Rather than running games itself, it streams them from your PC or Steam Deck with very little latency. It serves as a alternative to the Meta Quest headsets offered by Meta and is expected to recieve a following as it allows for native steam streaming in comparison to meta's bloated streaming experience.
